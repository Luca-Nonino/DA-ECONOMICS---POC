**Key Takeaways:**

- {**Unfilled Orders**}: [Unfilled orders rose by $3.1 billion, or 0.2%, to $1,386.3 billion, continuing an upward trend for forty-seven of the last forty-eight months. This increase follows a 1.4% decrease in June, indicating sustained demand that manufacturers are yet to fulfill.]
- {**Inventories**}: [Inventories increased by $0.6 billion, or 0.1%, to $859.4 billion in July, with increases in five of the last six months. This follows a 0.1% decrease in June, suggesting manufacturers are preparing for anticipated demand.]
- {**Ratios**}: [The unfilled orders-to-shipments ratio decreased to 6.76 from 6.91 in June, while the inventories-to-shipments ratio fell to 1.45 from 1.46, indicating a slight tightening in inventory relative to shipments, which may signal improved efficiency in the supply chain.]
