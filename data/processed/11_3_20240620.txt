**Key Takeaways:**
- {**Sales Volume**}: [Existing-home sales decreased by 1.9% from March to a seasonally adjusted annual rate of 4.14 million in April 2024. This is also a 1.9% decline from April 2023, indicating a consistent downward trend in sales volume.]
- {**Median Sales Price**}: [The median existing-home sales price increased by 5.7% year-over-year to $407,600, marking the tenth consecutive month of price gains and the highest price ever recorded for April.]
- {**Inventory Levels**}: [Total housing inventory rose by 9% from March to 1.21 million units, representing a 3.5-month supply at the current sales pace. This is a significant increase from the 3.0-month supply recorded in April 2023.]
- {**Regional Sales Trends**}: [Sales declined in the Northeast (4%), Midwest (1%), and South (1.6%) from March, but increased in the West by 1.3% year-over-year. The median prices in these regions also saw varying increases, with the West experiencing the highest rise at 9.3%.]
- {**First-Time Buyers**}: [First-time buyers accounted for 33% of sales in April, up from 32% in March and 29% in April 2023, indicating a growing participation of new entrants in the housing market.]
- {**All-Cash Sales**}: [All-cash transactions made up 28% of sales, consistent with March and April 2023, reflecting stable investor activity in the market.]
- {**Mortgage Rates**}: [The 30-year fixed-rate mortgage averaged 7.02% as of May 16, down slightly from 7.09% the previous week but up from 6.39% a year ago, impacting affordability and buyer decisions.]
