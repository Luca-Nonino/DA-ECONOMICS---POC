**Title:**
{'Unemployment Insurance Weekly Claims: Week Ending July 6, 2024'}

**Overview:**
||This report presents unemployment insurance claims data for the week ending July 6, 2024, with a focus on seasonally adjusted figures. The advance figure for seasonally adjusted initial claims decreased by 17,000 from the previous week, while the 4-week moving average also showed a decline. The seasonally adjusted insured unemployment rate remained unchanged at 1.2% for the week ending June 29. The total number of continued weeks claimed for benefits across all programs increased by 73,114 from the previous week.||

- {**Seasonally Adjusted Initial Claims**}: [The advance figure for seasonally adjusted initial claims was 222,000 for the week ending July 6, 2024, a decrease of 17,000 from the previous week's revised level of 239,000. This marks a significant downward trend in initial claims.]
- {**4-Week Moving Average**}: [The 4-week moving average for seasonally adjusted initial claims decreased to 233,500, a reduction of 5,250 from the previous week's revised average of 238,750. This average continues to trend downwards.]
- {**Seasonally Adjusted Insured Unemployment Rate**}: [The advance seasonally adjusted insured unemployment rate stood at 1.2% for the week ending June 29, 2024, unchanged from the previous week. This indicates a stabilization in the rate of insured unemployment.]
- {**Total Continued Weeks Claimed**}: [The total number of continued weeks claimed for benefits across all programs for the week ending June 22 was 1,845,164, representing a notable increase of 73,114 from the previous week. This suggests a potential short-term rise in unemployment.]
- {**State Variations**}: [The largest increases in initial claims for the week ending June 29 were observed in New York (+4,427), New Jersey (+2,557), Georgia (+1,849), California (+1,478), and Iowa (+1,270), while the most significant decreases occurred in Connecticut (-1,831), Wisconsin (-875), Minnesota (-731), Maryland (-690), and Vermont (-534).]
